Impact: Helps retrieval reliably match queries about UI elements and avoids returning chunks that refer to similarly named areas in other parts of the product.

Heading Hierarchy
Evidence: H2 'Use AI prompts to extract a summary' lacks the product/feature name while the body references 'Note Assist' and 'Dragon Copilot'
Action: Include the product or feature name and front‑load the discriminative task in the heading. For example, change to 'Note Assist: use AI prompts to extract a summary' and apply the same pattern to sibling headings that currently start 'Use AI prompts...'
Impact: Ensures H1+H2 prefixes contain a stable product scope plus a clear task, improving retrieval precision and reducing collisions among multiple headings that begin with 'Use AI prompts...'
Context Completeness
Evidence: 'based on the encounter summaries or transcript' and 'Dragon Copilot only searches the transcript and summary for the current encounter' (no guidance on creating or ensuring a transcript)
Action: Add a prerequisite line in this section: state that the current encounter must include a transcript or encounter summary before using Note Assist, and provide brief instructions or a link to generate or locate the transcript/summary.
Impact: Reduces user confusion and failed queries by making data requirements explicit, improving retrieval relevance when chunks indicate whether source content exists for AI prompts.
Structured Data
Evidence: Important: 'Dragon Copilot only searches the transcript and summary for the current encounter...' and examples show prompts but no expected output or verification
Action: Move the Important limitation to the top of this section as a concise 'limits' bullet (for example: 'Search scope: transcript and summary for the current encounter only; does not search EHR or past encounters'). Then add a one-line success check immediately after 'The response appears in the Timeline tab' that shows how to verify success (for example: 'Expected: a concise summary appears in Timeline under the current encounter with the heading "Note Assist summary"'). Also place one short sample expected output line next to the example prompts.
Impact: Makes the search scope and success criteria explicit and easy to find, preserving key anchors in each chunk so users and the retrieval system can reliably find limitations and how to verify results
Query-Answer Alignment
Evidence: Text says response appears in the Timeline tab and lists related topics but gives no instruction to insert or apply the summary into the note
Action: Add one front-loaded sentence under this heading that states whether users can apply or copy the generated summary into their note and, if applicable, briefly how to do it (for example, 'Use "Apply to note" in the Timeline or copy and paste the text'). If the step is documented in a related topic, add a direct inline link to that section.
Impact: Users who search for how to move AI-generated summaries into the note will get an immediate, extractable answer in the same chunk, reducing task friction and improving retrieval for insertion-related queries.
Redundancy Efficiency
Evidence: Related topics lists four sibling headings beginning with "Use AI prompts"
Action: Remove the repeated "Use AI prompts" prefix from the related-topic headings. Make each heading a concise, distinct phrase in sentence case that highlights the unique task (for example, "Queries", "Queries outside your note", "Edit your note", "Create supporting documents").
Impact: Reduces identical heading tokens across sibling chunks, improving result diversity so the retriever returns distinct, relevant chunks instead of many similar hits.
